Hi, I'm @solvaholic.

I'm a solvaholic. I need a problem to solve in much the same way an
alcoholic needs a drink. I learn by doing and understanding, so if you
want me to learn or do something, make it interactive and help me see
how it solves a problem we both care about.

A few things I try to live by:

- Seek opportunities to verify or correct what you know and believe.
  [Being wrong feels exactly like being right.](https://www.ted.com/talks/kathryn_schulz_on_being_wrong)
- We're each working with different, incomplete, and imperfect
  information. If you're going to fill in blanks, tell a story you
  enjoy.
- Effective communication is complicated and easy to mess up. If a
  [serial comma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serial_comma) eliminates
  ambiguity, use it.
- Communicate onymously. Anonymity is for when you don't feel safe being
  the messenger; the rest of the time, sign your name.

> *Truth is the edges of what you pay attention to.*

What drives me nuts: blame, closed-mindedness, and working without the
objective in mind. When I catch myself doing those, I try to correct it.
When I think someone else is, I'll say so or step away.
